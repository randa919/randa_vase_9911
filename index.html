Explore Collection

Welcome to the Artistry of Jingdezhen Porcelain

Welcome to Timeless Treasures, where the exquisite craftsmanship of Jingdezhen porcelain comes to life. This virtual museum showcases three iconic artifacts from China's Qing Dynasty, offering an immersive experience through detailed 3D models. Delve into the history, artistry, and cultural significance of these masterpieces, and explore the intricate designs that have captivated collectors and art lovers for centuries.

Featured Artifacts

Artifact 1: Qing Dynasty Covered Baluster Vase

This covered baluster vase is part of a five-piece garniture from the Qing dynasty, dating from the Kangxi reign (1662-1722). It is crafted from white porcelain with cobalt pigment under a clear glaze. The vase features a painted scene of a court lady being entertained by dancers and musicians, with floral decorations on the neck and a domed cover adorned with ladies, flowers, and rocks. Originally part of a collection owned by Mrs. B.F. Jones, it was gifted to the Freer Gallery of Art in 1980 by Myron S. Falk, Jr. and his sisters.

Artifact 2: Qing Dynasty Vase

This Qing Dynasty vase, circa 1700, originates from Jingdezhen, Jiangxi Province, China. Featuring a tall cylindrical shape with a narrow neck and outflaring rim, the vase is adorned with a mountain and lake scene painted in cobalt pigment beneath a clear glaze. It measures 26.3 cm in height and 11.7 cm in diameter, made of porcelain. Previously owned by Mrs. Julia B. Curtis of Williamsburg, VA, the vase was gifted to the Freer Gallery of Art in 1991 and is part of its collection.
